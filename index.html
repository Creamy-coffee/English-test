Section 3: Lecture

Script: Nearly everyone talks, but do you communicate? Learning the art of effective communication requires skill. Here are some top tips on becoming a more natural conversationalist and communicator. Communicating is easy for some people. They just seem to have a natural flair for conversation. They listen well and have a strong sense of confidence, or even a charisma, that endears them to others. But how do the rest of us learn how to communicate with others? Let's start by exploring the following easy-to-follow steps. Confidence: Good people skills start with confidence. When you have confidence, it shows. It attracts others to you like a magnet, because on some level, your self-confident attitude shows that you're worth other people's time and effort to get to know. A person with a strong sense of self tends to move quickly past the small talk to invite everyone into the heart of the conversation. A self-confident person makes others feel better about themselves. Show Interest: Many people make the mistake of talking too much about themselves. Nothing kills a conversation faster than rambling on about oneself. Having good communication skills means you have the ability to get others to talk about themselves, instead of making them listen to you. Showing interest in them helps the other person feel more at ease and boosts his or her confidence. It also creates a situation where the other person feels obligated to reciprocate. Talking about themselves for a while tends to make people want to return the favor by asking you a question and then listening as you speak. But remember to keep your end of the conversation short if you want it to continue. The Art of the Open-Ended Question: One of the quickest routes to an awkward silence is asking questions that can be answered yes or no. By asking a more open-ended questionâ€”something requiring a more detailed answerâ€”you encourage the other person to elaborate and offer more information. This makes them feel good about themselvesâ€”and about you, because you seem so interested in what they have to say.
